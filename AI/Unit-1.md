# 1) Introduction to Artificial Intelligence (AI)

[cite_start]AI is a branch of computer science focused on creating intelligent machines that can **behave like a human**, **think like humans**, and are able to **make decisions**[cite: 2048].

---

### 1. Core Definition and Concept
* [cite_start]**Composition:** AI is composed of $\text{Artificial}$ ("man-made") and $\text{Intelligence}$ ("thinking power")[cite: 2046].
* [cite_start]**Core Skills:** AI machines possess human-based skills such as **learning**, **reasoning**, and **solving problems**[cite: 2049].
* [cite_start]**Programming:** Instead of preprogramming a machine for every task, AI involves creating a machine with programmed algorithms that enable it to work with its **own intelligence**[cite: 2050, 2051].

### 6. History Milestones (Timeline)

[cite_start]The history defines the journey from the AI generation to present-day development[cite: 2115].

* [cite_start]**1943:** Warren McCulloch and Walter Pitts proposed a model of **artificial neurons**[cite: 2118].
* [cite_start]**1950:** Alan Turing published "Computing Machinery and Intelligence," proposing the **Turing test**[cite: 2120, 2121].
* [cite_start]**1956 (Birth of AI):** John McCarthy coined the term "**Artificial Intelligence**" at the Dartmouth Conference, establishing it as an academic field[cite: 2159, 2160].
* [cite_start]**1966:** Joseph Weizenbaum created the first chatbot, **ELIZA**[cite: 2165].
* [cite_start]**1972:** The first intelligent humanoid robot, **WABOT-1**, was built in Japan[cite: 2166].
* [cite_start]**1974-1980:** **First AI Winter** (Severe shortage of government funding for AI research)[cite: 2168, 2170].
* [cite_start]**1980:** AI came back with the introduction of **"Expert System"** (programs emulating human expert decision-making)[cite: 2173, 2174].
* [cite_start]**1987-1993:** **Second AI Winter** (Funding stopped due to high cost and inefficient results)[cite: 2177, 2178].
* [cite_start]**1997:** **IBM Deep Blue** became the first computer to beat a world chess champion (Gary Kasparov)[cite: 2181].
* [cite_start]**2002:** AI entered the home in the form of **Roomba** (a vacuum cleaner)[cite: 2182].
* [cite_start]**2011:** **IBM's Watson** won the Jeopardy quiz show, proving it could understand natural language and solve tricky questions[cite: 2185, 2186].
* [cite_start]**2014:** Chatbot **"Eugene Goostman"** won a competition in the Turing test[cite: 2188].
* [cite_start]**2018:** Google demonstrated **Duplex**, a virtual assistant that made calls without the person on the other end noticing they were talking to a machine[cite: 2190].
